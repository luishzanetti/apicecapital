## 0. The premise this round finally gets right

Apice Capital does not need a logo. It needs a **house style** — a mark, a wordmark, a typographic voice, a motion signature, and a color discipline that, seen together, could not have been made by anyone else.

Hermès is not a horse-and-carriage. Hermès is: the mark, the **HERMÈS PARIS** wordmark with its signature open "R," the saddle-stitch seam as a brand element, the orange boxes, and the editorial typography that runs the window displays. Five things doing one thing. Strip any one out and it is no longer Hermès.

Rolex is not a crown. Rolex is: the 5-pointed crown with the specific concave valleys, the "ROLEX" wordmark with its signature tight kerning and flat-footed serifs on the C and E, the green seal of authenticity, and an unchanging font hierarchy across every dial. Again — five things doing one thing.

Apice has been asking for a logo. Apice needs a house. This document delivers two candidate houses.

Each of the two directions below is a **complete brand system**. Each walks a specific heritage triangle. Each can sit on a moodboard next to Hermès, Cartier, Rolex, Stripe, and Linear without being the weakest entry. One of them is what Apice ships. Both are defended with conviction.

## 1. Direction One — **CIFRA** (Italian for *cipher / figure / numeral*)

> The wordmark is the logo. The seal is the stamp. The ledger is the product.

**Thesis:** Apice is an Italian word. Lean into it. Build a system that reads as a modern descendant of Florentine banking — the Medici account book reborn as software. The signature is the **custom wordmark with a ligature**, and the second mark is a **horizontal seal** that behaves like a stamp of authenticity, not a symbol you decorate with.

### 1.1 Heritage Reference Triangle — Hermès × Cartier × Stripe

| Reference | What CIFRA borrows |
|---|---|
| **Hermès** | The wordmark-plus-seal duality. "HERMÈS PARIS" locked beneath the carriage is a system, not a logo. CIFRA borrows the *stacked authority* — wordmark as the face, seal as the anchor. Also: Hermès caps are loose, not industrial. CIFRA respects that — the caps breathe. |
| **Cartier** | Custom cursive energy inside a display-caps logotype. Cartier looks drawn, not typeset. CIFRA's "AP" ligature and the lowered crossbar on the "A" channel the same hand-cut intent without copying the cursive. |
| **Stripe** | The idea that the wordmark is stronger than the icon. Stripe's most identifiable asset is the custom "S" in their wordmark, not the /S/ favicon. CIFRA bets the farm on the wordmark the same way. |

### 1.2 Strategic Rationale

**Paragraph 1 — Why the wordmark, not the symbol, carries this system.** The current crop of crypto-adjacent marks — Kraken's K, Coinbase's blue "C," Nexo's gold "N" — all rely on the monogram inside a colored square to do the lifting. That is a *fintech startup* pattern, not a *wealth institution* pattern. Wealth institutions (Rothschild, Pictet, Lombard Odier, Hermès, Cartier) lead with the wordmark and treat the mark as an ancillary stamp. CIFRA adopts that posture. When a Club member ($149.90/month) sees "APICE CAPITAL" set in the custom face, the weight is in the letters themselves — not in a little decorated square.

**Paragraph 2 — Why italics-adjacent, not fully italic.** A fully italic wordmark (Barclays Wealth, certain European private banks) reads as *legacy.* A pure upright sans (Coinbase, Robinhood) reads as *startup.* CIFRA sits between — caps that are optically upright at glance, but each letter has an internal forward-lean of 2° to 4° built into specific counters and terminals. This is the same trick Cartier uses: the word reads upright, but the *energy* is forward. Forward-motion without performative movement. That is Apice's thesis of wealth — discipline that compounds, not flash that flexes.

**Paragraph 3 — Why CIFRA survives five years.** Italic-adjacent display caps are a 400-year-old design idea (Renaissance humanist capitals through to modern luxury). This is not a 2020s trend. In five years, a warm indigo gradient will look dated. In five years, a custom cap-height wordmark with a deliberate ligature will look like *Apice finally settled into itself.* The system accrues equity rather than decays.

**Paragraph 4 — Why CIFRA fits the Club tier economically.** The $149.90/month user is paying for **judgment** — that Apice saw what they could not, and their money is in adult hands. CIFRA's wordmark visually *is* that judgment. It does not need to prove anything; it is already proof. The Robinhood green square cannot carry that message at $149.90. The Medici ledger inheritance can.

### 1.3 The Mark — The Apice Seal

**Construction:** A horizontal rule sandwich. Top rule, centered monogram "A," bottom rule. The marks frame a single custom "A." Total aspect ratio 3:1 (wide, stamp-like, never square).

**Grid (in u units, where 1u = logo x-height / 10):**

```
     ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
     │                                          │   ← top rule: 30u wide × 0.6u thick
     └──────────────────────────────────────────┘
                       /\                           
                      /  \                          ← A glyph: 9u wide × 10u tall
                     /    \                         
                    /──────\                        ← crossbar at 35% height
                   /        \                       
                  /          \                      
                 └            ┘                     ← feet: flat, 0.4u stub outward
     ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
     │                                          │   ← bottom rule: 30u wide × 0.6u thick
     └──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Proportions:**
- Rules: 30u × 0.6u, matched top and bottom. Always pair.
- "A" centered: 9u wide × 10u tall.
- The apex of the "A" is **flat**, not pointed — a 1.8u flat plateau. This is the defining move. The "A" that is *cut like a column capital*, not *drawn like a mountain.*
- Crossbar: 35% up from baseline (lower than a geometric sans default of 50%). The lower crossbar widens the top chamber and gives the mark an *editorial, chiseled* feel rather than a geometric-tech feel.
- Feet: 0.4u flat stubs on each side of the baseline. Subtle — invisible at 16px, unmistakable at 128px.

**Craft detail (the one to be proud of):** The inner bowl of the "A" — the negative triangle above the crossbar — is **not** a perfect triangle. Its apex is offset rightward by 0.15u, producing a subtle forward-lean that echoes the wordmark's 3° energy. At 24px this is imperceptible. At 96px and above, a trained eye reads it as *intentional*. This is the detail that separates *designed* from *generated.*

**Optical correction:** The top rule is 0.05u lighter than the bottom rule (0.55u vs. 0.60u). Gravity pulls the eye down — equal weights would read as top-heavy. This is a terminal engraver's trick used on the Rolex crown.

**Usage:** The seal is the *favicon, the app icon glyph, the stamp on certificates, the AI Expert avatar frame, the onboarding completion seal.* It never appears without purpose. It is never decorative. It is always a *mark of authenticity* — which is precisely why it earns the name Seal.

### 1.4 The Custom Wordmark — *APICE CAPITAL*

**Base:** Geist Display at SemiBold is the starting point — then half the letters are redrawn.

**Custom moves (the seven adjustments):**

1. **"A" (both instances):** flat apex matching the seal's "A." 1.8u plateau. Crossbar at 35%, not 50%. This is the signature letter.
2. **"P":** the bowl does not fully close — there is a 0.25u gap where the bowl meets the stem. This is a **subtle aperture** that echoes Cartier's open bowls. Unreadable as a gap at any functional size; visible only as *softness* to the eye.
3. **"I":** bare stem with flat terminals (no dots, no ornaments). The "I" provides the visual pause between "AP" and "CE."
4. **"C" (both instances):** the terminals of the "C" are cut on a 5° bias, not a horizontal cut. This reads as a *pen stroke* rather than a laser cut — humanist warmth inside geometric caps.
5. **"E":** the middle arm of the "E" is 0.5u shorter than the top and bottom arms. The "E" has a subtle left-lean in weight distribution. This is the move Stripe made on their custom "e."
6. **"T":** the crossbar terminals are cut on the same 5° bias as the "C." Consistency of hand.
7. **"AP" ligature (signature move):** In the stacked lockup, "A" and "P" share a terminal — the right foot of the "A" flows into the left stem of the "P" via a 1.2u bridge that looks like a serif but is a joining stroke. This ligature appears ONLY in the primary "APICE" wordmark, never in body copy. It is the equivalent of the knot in the Hermès logo — tiny, specific, impossible to mistake.

**Letter widths (proprietary):**
A = 8.5u · P = 6.8u · I = 2.0u · C = 7.4u · E = 6.2u (spaces between 0.8u to 1.0u depending on letter pair)

**Tracking:**
- "APICE" display: +120 units (tracked out, breathing)
- "CAPITAL" subtitle: +280 units (further tracked, smaller, all-caps)
- Body setting (never Apice wordmark in body): default Geist tracking

**Optical adjustments:**
- "APICE" caps sit 0.8u above the true baseline to optically center against "CAPITAL" subtitle
- The "P" in "CAPITAL" subtitle has its bowl closed (no aperture) — the aperture is reserved for the primary "APICE" wordmark, not the secondary "CAPITAL" subtitle. This creates a *hierarchy of craft* where the primary word is more custom than the secondary.

**Why the wordmark is as much a mark as the seal is:** Strip the seal entirely. "APICE" in the custom face is still unmistakably Apice. Set the word in any other sans and it evaporates. The letters are the brand equity. The seal is the *punctuation* of the brand.

### 1.5 The Lockup

**Primary stacked lockup (certificates, splash, hero moments):**

```
       ┌────────────────────────────────┐
       │                                │    ← seal (top rule)
       └────────────────────────────────┘

                  /─\
                 /   \                         ← seal (A glyph, flat apex)
                /     \
               /───────\
              /         \
             └           ┘

       ┌────────────────────────────────┐
       │                                │    ← seal (bottom rule)
       └────────────────────────────────┘

              A P I C E                       ← wordmark: custom caps
              ─────────
              C A P I T A L                   ← subtitle: tracked caps
```

**Proportional rules (stacked):**
- Seal width = 100% of "APICE" wordmark width
- Vertical gap between seal's bottom rule and "APICE" baseline = 1.5× the x-height of APICE
- "APICE" to "CAPITAL" gap = 0.6× x-height
- Thin hairline (0.4u) separates APICE from CAPITAL — Hermès trick, quiet authority

**Horizontal lockup (headers, email signatures, press):**

```
   ┌─────┐
   │  A  │    A P I C E    ·    C A P I T A L
   └─────┘
```

- Seal at 1× wordmark cap-height, sits left
- Wordmark right-aligned starting 1.2× cap-height from seal's right edge
- "APICE" and "CAPITAL" separated by a middle dot (·), never pipe or em-dash
- The dot is 0.8u diameter, sits at optical center of the cap-height (not x-height)

**Single-line compact (nav bars, 24px contexts):**

```
   A · APICE CAPITAL
```

- Just the "A" glyph + dot + wordmark, wordmark tracked tighter (+60 units)
- Used where vertical space is 24–32px

### 1.6 Typography System

**Display face:** Geist Display at SemiBold (600) for the custom wordmark. Custom letters drawn on top of Geist's skeleton.

**Product face:** Geist Sans, three committed weights only:
- **Regular (400):** body copy, long-form, Academy content
- **Medium (500):** UI labels, button text, row headers
- **Semibold (600):** headlines, section titles, financial figures

**Numeric face:** Geist Mono for all financial figures. Tabular lining numerals, no proportional. Every "$1,249.00" in the app is Geist Mono, every time.

**Editorial face (rare, ceremonial):** Signature use of **Geist Display Black (900)** for initiation moments only — the Apice Code Manifesto headers, Club welcome, Elite tier unlock. Black is the *ceremonial weight* — it appears perhaps 6 times across the entire product. This scarcity makes it matter.

**Forbidden in this system:** Geist Thin and ExtraLight. Wealth does not whisper; it is quiet but weighted. No weights below 400 anywhere.

### 1.7 Color Application

**Mandatory monochrome contexts:**
- The seal, in all contexts, is always *single-color.* Either warm indigo on cream, or cream on warm indigo. Never split, never gradient, never duotone.
- The wordmark is always single-color. Same palette rule.
- Financial figures are always *neutral-900* (near-black, warm-tinted). Never indigo, never amber.

**Where color is load-bearing:**
- Warm Indigo `#8B5CF6`: primary brand surface. Used as background on the Club tier marketing site, on the Apice Code Manifesto page, on the Elite tier dashboard. When indigo appears, it signals *this is Apice's ground.*
- Warm Cream `#F7F3ED` (warm off-white, not pure white): the neutral ground of all product surfaces. Never pure `#FFFFFF`. This single decision is 30% of the warmth differentiation.
- Warm Amber `#F59E0B`: **ceremonial only.** Appears on celebration moments, on the Apice Code Manifesto illuminated capitals, on completion seals, on Founding Member badges. Never on a button. Never on a link. Never as a default. Amber is the *gold leaf* of this system — precious because rare.

**Color hierarchy principle:** *Indigo is the house. Cream is the room. Amber is the candle.*

### 1.8 Motion — The Seal Draws Itself

**Signature animation (one and only one):** On splash and on Apice Code ceremony moments, the seal draws in the following order over 1800ms:

1. **0–400ms:** Top rule draws left-to-right (300ms) with a gentle ease-out (cubic-bezier 0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)
2. **400–900ms:** Bottom rule draws *right-to-left* (mirror direction — this is the signature detail; it feels like a ledger closing)
3. **900–1400ms:** The "A" glyph draws as a single continuous pen-stroke: left stem up, flat apex across, right stem down, then the crossbar draws left-to-right
4. **1400–1800ms:** The wordmark "APICE CAPITAL" fades in at 0.85 opacity, then settles to 1.0

The subtlety is in the *reversed bottom-rule direction.* It is unexpected, it reads as **deliberate craft**, and it frames the glyph as being *drawn into an existing ledger entry* rather than appearing from nothing. This is the motion equivalent of Hermès' saddle-stitch — specific enough to be a signature, quiet enough to never scream.

**Where this motion appears:**
- First launch splash (once per install, skippable after first time)
- Apice Code Manifesto page load (always)
- Elite tier unlock celebration (once per user, permanent memory)
- Club welcome email hero (GIF fallback, 24fps)

Nowhere else. Motion is precious.

### 1.9 Extension to Product — The Ledger Bullet

**Specific example:** In the Apice Code Manifesto (the tattoo-worthy document the CEO is polishing), the tenets are listed as a numbered series. Standard bullet points would be fine. Instead, the bullet for each tenet is a **miniature seal rule** — a 14u × 0.6u horizontal line sitting at cap-height of the tenet heading. No number, no bullet, no icon. Just the rule.

```
     ───────     Wealth is a practice, not an event.
                 Consistency is the compound we own...

     ───────     Risk is a feature, not a flaw.
                 We name what we cannot control...

     ───────     Time is the market's only fair exchange.
                 We trade patience for returns...
```

This does three things simultaneously:
1. Makes the Manifesto visually coherent with the seal (the rule IS the seal top)
2. Introduces the ledger aesthetic without literal ledger imagery
3. Produces a deeply reusable motif — the same rule becomes section dividers in Academy, the separator between AI Expert cards, the frame for Club-tier price chips

The **ledger rule** becomes Apice's equivalent of the Hermès saddle-stitch: a single geometric primitive that recurs across the product and always reads as Apice's signature.

### 1.10 Craft Audit — Five Details That Reward Inspection

1. **The flat-apex "A"** with crossbar at 35%, offset inner triangle, forward-leaning 0.15u. This is the letter the Roman stone-cutters carved. Present in both the seal and the wordmark, and nowhere else in the product.
2. **The mismatched top/bottom rules** (0.55u vs. 0.60u) — a 9% weight differential to correct optical gravity. Invisible unless you look for it. The kind of detail that appears in engraver's handbooks, not logo templates.
3. **The "AP" ligature** in the stacked wordmark only — a 1.2u joining stroke that does not appear in any other context. One ligature, one home. Hermès-level scarcity.
4. **The 5°-biased "C" and "T" terminals** — all other letters have horizontal terminals. Only C and T are cut at a bias, channeling pen-stroke warmth inside geometric caps. A trained eye spots this at 40px.
5. **The reversed bottom-rule motion** — the bottom rule draws right-to-left while the top draws left-to-right. Motion craft as signature. This is the kind of detail interaction designers screenshot and tweet about.

### 1.11 What CIFRA Refuses to Be

*(Leading with what it is, not what it rejects — but for completeness.)*
- Not a monogram-in-a-square (Coinbase, Kraken, Nexo pattern)
- Not baroque luxury with ornament stacks (Nexo maximalist gold)
- Not italic-as-performance (European private banks 1980s)
- Not Swiss-modernist-cold (Relai, UBS)
- Not crypto-degen (no neon, no glow, no "APICE" in wide display-black)

### 1.12 Production Honesty

**Complete system delivery (mark + wordmark + lockups + app icon family + favicon + motion spec + typography spec):**

- **Days 1–2:** Seal construction — vectorize the "A" with all optical corrections, both rules with weight differential, grid documentation
- **Days 3–5:** Wordmark — redraw seven custom letters on the Geist skeleton, test at every size from 12px to 1200px, build the ligature variant
- **Days 6–7:** Lockup system — stacked, horizontal, compact variants. All three tested in real contexts (app splash, email signature, press release header, favicon)
- **Days 8–9:** App icon family — iOS (rounded-square), Android (adaptive), macOS (squircle with safe-area), favicon (16/32/64px), Apple Touch Icon (180px). Each needs specific optical tuning; a 16px favicon is not a resized 1024px icon.
- **Day 10:** Motion spec — the seal-draw animation with precise easing curves, Lottie export, fallback GIF, fallback static
- **Day 11:** Typography spec document — the three-weight commitment, numeric face, ceremonial black usage rules
- **Day 12:** QA and live-context testing on actual product surfaces

**Total: 12 working days** (2.5 weeks) for a complete, shippable system. This is realistic. Luxury-grade craft takes this time; anything less is template work.

## 2. Direction Two — **CAPITELLO** (Italian for *column capital*, the crowning piece of a Corinthian column)

> A monumental wordmark on a monumental grid. The mark is the word and the word is a monument.

**Thesis:** If CIFRA is the Medici ledger, CAPITELLO is the Roman Senate. Apice shipped as architecture — letters carved like Trajan's column, a mark that is effectively a miniature inscription panel. Where CIFRA leans private-banking-warm, CAPITELLO leans civic-institutional-authoritative. Both are premium; they are premium in different registers.

### 2.1 Heritage Reference Triangle — Rolex × Linear × Apple

| Reference | What CAPITELLO borrows |
|---|---|
| **Rolex** | The monumental wordmark. ROLEX is a word carved on a dial, not drawn on a monitor. CAPITELLO treats APICE the same way — every letter weighted, kerned, and proportioned for *inscription*, not screen-reading. |
| **Linear** | The software-native discipline. Linear's wordmark (linear.app) has perfect optical corrections, a famous signature "a" with a cut terminal, and a typographic voice that unifies mark, product UI, and marketing. CAPITELLO adopts Linear's system-wide rigor — the same letters live in the logo, the product nav, and the Academy headers. |
| **Apple** | The restraint of the single mark. Apple's apple is one silhouette that carries the entire brand. CAPITELLO's mark — the *capital notch* glyph — is equally restrained, equally single-shape, but comes paired with a monumental wordmark so it never has to carry alone. |

### 2.2 Strategic Rationale

**Paragraph 1 — Why monumental, and why now.** The current premium category has over-indexed on *small and neat* (Stripe, Linear, Vercel, Railway). There is a lane opening for *large and carved* — a return to the weight of institutional lettering without the stuffiness of old wealth. Think Trajan's column updated with a designer's eye. Apice occupies this lane with zero competitors. Nexo tries it with baroque gold and fails into pastiche. CAPITELLO succeeds because the monumentality is in the *letter construction*, not in ornament.

**Paragraph 2 — Why the wordmark must be the primary brand asset.** Apice's name is Italian for *apex*. It has vowel-consonant rhythm (A-P-I-C-E — five characters, three syllables, perfectly balanced). It is phonetically distinctive and visually balanced. A wordmark that leans into this rhythm — A and E as monumental anchors, I as the central pause, P and C as the tension — becomes its own mark. CAPITELLO treats the word as a monument and the mark as the chisel that carved it.

**Paragraph 3 — Why this system commands $149.90/month.** A Club subscriber opens the app. The splash resolves. What they see is a word the size of their palm, carved like the top of a Corinthian column, with craft that a designer would not notice at first glance but would recognize on second. That craft is the receipt. The user did not just buy a subscription — they bought entry into a product that was *cared for at the level of the letters.* This is the same message Cartier delivers when you open the box. The box is not the watch. The *care of the box* is the evidence of the watch.

**Paragraph 4 — Why CAPITELLO survives the five-year horizon.** Monumental inscription lettering is 2,000 years old. Apple's Myriad-adjacent wordmark from 1984 still works in 2026. Rolex's wordmark from the 1930s is unchanged. CAPITELLO's construction is not chasing a 2020s aesthetic — it is channeling a canon that long predates design trends. The system ages into itself; it does not age out.

### 2.3 The Mark — The Capitello Glyph

**Construction:** A single, small glyph — the **notched "A" standing between two horizontal rules** — but compressed into a single tight rectangle, not the horizontal stamp format of CIFRA. The mark is 1:1 aspect ratio (square-fitted, unlike CIFRA's 3:1 stamp).

**Grid (in u units):**

```
    ┌─────────────────┐
    │  ───────────    │   ← top rule: 10u × 0.7u, sitting 2u below top edge
    │                 │
    │       /\        │
    │      /  \       │   ← A glyph: 6u × 8u
    │     /────\      │   ← crossbar at 35%
    │    /      \     │
    │   └        ┘    │
    │                 │
    │  ───────────    │   ← bottom rule: 10u × 0.7u, sitting 2u above bottom edge
    └─────────────────┘
```

**Where CAPITELLO differs from CIFRA:**
- The marks are **heavier** (0.7u vs CIFRA's 0.6u). CAPITELLO reads as inscription; CIFRA reads as ledger.
- The marks are **shorter** (10u vs 30u wide). Compressed into a square format. This lets the mark live as a true icon at favicon sizes — CIFRA's 3:1 format is too horizontal for a 16px favicon.
- The "A" is **bolder** in construction — stems 1.2u thick (vs CIFRA's 1.0u). This is the difference between *chiseled into stone* and *written with a pen*.
- The **flat apex is wider** — 2.2u plateau (vs CIFRA's 1.8u). Stone-cut quality rather than pen-cut quality.

**Craft detail:** At the base of each A-stem, there is a **1.0u serif stub** — not a full serif like Trajan, but a proto-serif. The stub says "this letter sits on a ground." Present at display sizes (>96px), optically blended out at small sizes (<48px) via weighted rounding. This is a **size-responsive detail** that makes the mark feel heavier at the display size where it is most likely to be inspected.

**Usage:** Unlike CIFRA's seal (which is a *stamp*), the Capitello glyph is an *emblem*. It appears as the primary app icon, favicon, AI Expert tier badge (the Expert avatars sit *inside* the glyph frame), Club member certificate insignia, and marketing hero asset. It is permitted to appear alone; the wordmark does not need to chaperone.

### 2.4 The Custom Wordmark — *APICE CAPITAL*

**Base:** Geist Display at Black (900) is the starting point — CAPITELLO runs heavier than CIFRA. Then every letter is redrawn.

**Custom moves (the eight adjustments — CAPITELLO is *more* custom than CIFRA):**

1. **"A" (both instances):** flat apex at 2.2u plateau (matching the glyph), crossbar at 35% height. Stems 1.2u thick. Slight ink-trap at apex/stem junction — a 0.3u chamfer that only appears above 120px. Below that, the junction renders as a clean corner.
2. **"P":** bowl closed (unlike CIFRA's open aperture). CAPITELLO is about solidity; CIFRA is about openness. The "P" bowl is *geometrically* perfect but *optically* adjusted — the bowl's widest point is 0.1u offset above geometric center to correct for perceived top-heaviness.
3. **"I":** a **monolithic slab** — 1.2u stem with flat caps, no serifs at all. The "I" in CAPITELLO is a column section; it is not a line.
4. **"C" (both instances):** terminals are **flat and horizontal** (unlike CIFRA's 5° bias). CAPITELLO's letters are all orthogonal — no biases, no humanist warmth. The warmth comes from the *weight*, not the terminals. The "C" aperture (the opening) is 45° wide, wider than a default sans — this is the *stone-cut* opening.
5. **"E":** all three arms are the same length (unlike CIFRA's shortened middle arm). Full equality. The stem is 1.2u thick, arms are 0.9u (lighter than stems — classical lettering proportion).
6. **"T":** the crossbar extends 1.8u past each side of the stem — wider than a default sans. This is the *column capital* move — a crossbar that reads as a *capital* sitting on the stem.
7. **"L":** the foot extends 2.0u past the stem. Wider than the T crossbar. This creates a visual *stepping-down* pattern in "CAPITAL" — T is wide, L is wider. Subtle but felt.
8. **No ligatures.** Unlike CIFRA, CAPITELLO has no ligatures. Every letter stands alone as its own monument. The *system* is in the weight and proportion, not in connections.

**Letter widths (proprietary):**
A = 10.0u · P = 8.0u · I = 2.2u · C = 8.8u · E = 7.4u (spaces: 1.2u between letters, 2.0u between words)

**Tracking:**
- "APICE" display: +60 units (tighter than CIFRA — CAPITELLO letters are heavier and need less air)
- "CAPITAL" subtitle: +200 units
- Word space: 3.5x normal — wide, giving air to each word

**Optical adjustments:**
- Cap-height is 0.3u above the true baseline on A, P, C, E (optical centering against the I, which sits on true baseline)
- The bowl of the P is 0.1u above geometric center (corrects perceived top-heaviness)
- The crossbar of the T is visually aligned to A's flat apex when stacked in a lockup

**Signature move:** The weight itself is the signature. Where CIFRA whispers "we cut these letters for you," CAPITELLO says "we carved these letters for you." The monumentality — 1.2u stems in an era of 0.6u hairlines — is the statement.

**Why the wordmark is as much a mark as the glyph is:** Remove the glyph. "APICE" in CAPITELLO weight, set in isolation on a warm cream background, is a **full inscription.** The word functions as a monument. This is the same test Rolex passes: set ROLEX in isolation and it still reads as Rolex. Apice in CAPITELLO passes the same test.

### 2.5 The Lockup

**Primary stacked lockup (splash, certificates, hero moments):**

```
    │                 │

    │   └        ┘    │
    │                 │
    │  ───────────    │
    └─────────────────┘

         A  P  I  C  E                ← wordmark, tracked
         ─────────────                 ← hairline divider (1.2u thick)
         C A P I T A L                 ← subtitle, tracked further
```

**Proportional rules (stacked):**
- Glyph width = 0.4× "APICE" wordmark width (glyph is smaller, wordmark dominates)
- Vertical gap between glyph bottom and "APICE" cap-height = 2.0× APICE cap-height
- Hairline divider between APICE and CAPITAL is **1.2u thick** (heavier than CIFRA's — monumentality throughout)

**Horizontal lockup (headers, press):**

```
   ┌─────┐   A  P  I  C  E   C A P I T A L
   │  A  │
   └─────┘
```

- Glyph at 1.2× APICE cap-height (slightly taller than caps, anchoring the row)
- APICE and CAPITAL separated by 2.0× cap-height word space — no divider dot, no middle symbol. The weight separates them; ornament would undermine.

**Single-line compact:**

```
    ▢  APICE
```

- Glyph (as solid square silhouette) + wordmark
- Used in 24–32px contexts where the fine detail of the glyph cannot render

### 2.6 Typography System

**Display face:** Geist Display at Black (900) for the custom wordmark — heavy, monumental. Custom letters drawn on top.

**Product face:** Geist Sans, three committed weights:
- **Regular (400):** body
- **Medium (500):** UI labels
- **Bold (700):** headlines (heavier than CIFRA's Semibold — CAPITELLO is monumental throughout)

**Numeric face:** Geist Mono, same as CIFRA — tabular, consistent decimals.

**Editorial face:** Geist Display Black (900) is the *default* display for CAPITELLO (unlike CIFRA where Black is ceremonial). The Apice Code Manifesto in CAPITELLO reads like a Roman inscription — all Black caps, tracked +200, on a warm cream ground. Monumentality is the *system*, not a special case.

**Forbidden in this system:** Any weight below Medium (500). No Regular for display. No Light anywhere. CAPITELLO is committed to heft.

### 2.7 Color Application

**Same palette as CIFRA (Warm Indigo + Warm Amber + Warm Cream), different ratios:**

- **Warm Cream `#F7F3ED` is dominant** — 70% of any given surface. CAPITELLO's monumentality needs negative space to breathe.
- **Warm Indigo `#8B5CF6` is structural** — used for the wordmark on light grounds, and as the full-bleed ground for ceremony moments (Club dashboard, Apice Code page).
- **Warm Amber `#F59E0B` is ceremonial** — same rule as CIFRA. Reserved for completion seals, Founding Member badges, Elite tier unlock, and illuminated capitals in the Manifesto.

**Key color move unique to CAPITELLO:** **Warm Indigo ink on Warm Cream paper** is the primary composition. This is the *carved inscription* pairing — the indigo reads like deep ink on thick paper, and the cream reads like uncoated fine stock. Nothing pure-white, nothing black. The palette evokes *letterpress on Crane's stationery*, which is exactly the register for $149.90/month.

**Color hierarchy principle:** *Cream is the stone. Indigo is the chisel. Amber is the gilt.*

### 2.8 Motion — The Weight Settles

**Signature animation:** On splash and ceremony moments, the wordmark materializes as follows over 1600ms:

1. **0–200ms:** Cream ground holds steady (intentional stillness — CAPITELLO earns its reveal)
2. **200–700ms:** "APICE" letters fade in individually in sequence A→P→I→C→E, each letter at 60ms offset, each fading from 0.0 to 1.0 at cubic-bezier (0.33, 1, 0.68, 1) — a settling ease, not a bouncing ease. Each letter *arrives* rather than *appears.*
3. **700–1100ms:** The glyph (Capitello emblem) scales in from 0.92 to 1.00 with opacity 0 → 1, same cubic-bezier
4. **1100–1600ms:** "CAPITAL" subtitle fades in as a block (not letter-by-letter — the subtitle is a *confirmation*, not a reveal), then a hairline of 1.2u thickness draws between APICE and CAPITAL over the final 300ms

The signature detail is the **letter-sequence fade** — APICE letters arrive individually. This reads as the word being *set in stone letter by letter*, echoing the inscription metaphor. The 60ms offset is deliberate; faster (40ms) reads as a nervous typewriter; slower (80ms) reads as an elevator. 60ms is *confident settling.*

**Where this motion appears:** Same four contexts as CIFRA (launch splash, Manifesto page, Elite unlock, Club welcome). Motion is precious.

### 2.9 Extension to Product — The Capital Column

**Specific example:** Apice's product navigation has seven primary sections (Dashboard, DCA, Altis, Academy, Apice Code, Club, Settings). In CAPITELLO, the active-state indicator is not an underline or a background fill — it is a **1.2u vertical rule** on the left edge of the active nav item, full-height, in warm indigo. The rule is the *column capital's* vertical stroke — a literal column marking a chosen path.

```
   │  Dashboard
      DCA
   │  Altis           ← active: full-height 1.2u indigo rule
      Academy
      Apice Code
      Club
      Settings
```

The rule is the system motif. It appears:
- As the active-state nav indicator
- As the divider between sections in the Apice Code Manifesto
- As the frame edge of Elite tier certificates (two rules top and bottom, a single rule on each side — forming an inscription frame)
- As the left edge of block-quoted AI Expert commentary in the Academy
- As the tick-mark on the DCA chart's commitment threshold

**The 1.2u rule** is CAPITELLO's equivalent of the Hermès saddle-stitch — one primitive, infinitely reusable, always Apice.

### 2.10 Craft Audit — Five Details That Reward Inspection

1. **The size-responsive A-stem serif stub** — 1.0u stubs appear above 96px, dissolve below. This is the mark of a system that was designed for *every size*, not a flat SVG scaled up and down.
2. **The 0.1u offset bowl on the P** — geometrically wrong by 0.1u, optically correct. Only a typographer spots this; only a CEO who spotted it would hire the designer.
3. **The stepped-width L > T > E in "CAPITAL"** — each letter wider than the last in its extensions (E arms at 0.9u, T crossbar extending 1.8u, L foot extending 2.0u). A visual rhythm inside a four-letter word. Reads as *deliberate rhythm* even to untrained eyes.
4. **The letter-sequence fade motion** — APICE letters arrive individually at 60ms offsets. Nobody screenshots this. Everybody *feels* it.
5. **The 1.2u rule as system primitive** — the same thickness governs the lockup divider, the nav active-state, the certificate frame, and the Manifesto dividers. One number governs five interfaces. This is **system coherence** made visible.

### 2.11 What CAPITELLO Refuses to Be

*(For record.)*
- Not tech-sans-generic (Stripe/Linear at their most minimal — CAPITELLO has more weight)
- Not luxury-maximalist (Nexo, gilded European private banks — CAPITELLO has more restraint)
- Not Apple-invisible (Apple recedes; CAPITELLO *occupies*)
- Not crypto-modern (no gradient, no glow, no decorative elements)
- Not Swiss-austere (Relai reads as cold; CAPITELLO is warm-through-weight)

### 2.12 Production Honesty

**Complete system delivery (mark + wordmark + lockups + app icon family + favicon + motion spec + typography spec):**

- **Days 1–3:** Capitello glyph construction — the size-responsive stubs require building three optical size masters (small/medium/display), each manually tuned. Heavier wordmarks need more optical work than lighter ones.
- **Days 4–7:** Wordmark — redrawing eight custom letters at Black weight. Heavier letters take longer than lighter letters — every ink-trap and junction needs individual tuning. The P's offset bowl alone takes half a day.
- **Days 8–9:** Lockup system — stacked, horizontal, compact variants. CAPITELLO's monumentality requires more negative space tuning than CIFRA; lockups that work at 400px may fail at 40px, and vice versa.
- **Days 10–11:** App icon family — iOS, Android adaptive, macOS, favicon at 16/32/64, Apple Touch Icon. The Capitello glyph's square format (1:1) is friendlier to iconography than CIFRA's 3:1 stamp.
- **Day 12:** Motion spec — the letter-sequence fade requires precise timing curves and Lottie export. The 60ms offset is the project's opinionated choice; getting it right takes the full day.
- **Day 13:** Typography spec document — the three-weight commitment, Black as default display, numeric face.
- **Day 14:** QA across 12 real product contexts (splash, nav, onboarding hero, Club dashboard, AI Expert card, Academy section divider, Manifesto capitals, DCA chart tick, Elite unlock, email signature, favicon, App Store screenshot).

**Total: 14 working days** (3 weeks) for a complete, shippable system. CAPITELLO takes slightly longer than CIFRA because the heavier weight and size-responsive details require more optical masters.

## 3. Comparison — The Four Tests

### 3.1 System Integrity Test — *Which coheres better as a complete system?*

**Both cohere strongly**, because both were designed as systems rather than isolated marks. The differentiator:

- **CIFRA** has more *visible connection* between its elements — the ledger rule appears everywhere, the seal is a literal stamp, the "A" is the same letter in the mark and the wordmark. The system is self-referential.
- **CAPITELLO** has more *tonal coherence* than literal connection. The 1.2u rule is the primitive, but the weight of every letter and every surface carries the system. The system is atmospheric.

**Edge: CIFRA.** The ledger rule gives CIFRA a single primitive that the designer can point to and say *this is Apice.* CAPITELLO requires the full context to read as Apice; strip the context, and the weight alone may read as generic-premium. CIFRA's self-reference is stronger.

### 3.2 Heritage Test — *Which holds up on a moodboard with Hermès / Cartier / Rolex?*

- **CIFRA** sits naturally next to Hermès and Cartier. The wordmark-plus-seal duality is directly in their lineage. The AP ligature channels Cartier. The italic-lean channels mid-century Hermès.
- **CAPITELLO** sits naturally next to Rolex. The monumental carved wordmark *is* the Rolex register. Less natural next to Hermès (which leans more cursive-humanist than CAPITELLO's inscriptional weight).

**Edge: Tie, depending on which heritage brand matters most.** If Apice's aspiration is *private-bank warm heritage*, CIFRA wins. If Apice's aspiration is *monumental civic authority*, CAPITELLO wins. Both are defensible heritage-grade systems.

### 3.3 Contemporary Test — *Which holds up with Stripe / Linear?*

- **CIFRA's wordmark-primacy** is in Stripe's register — a custom wordmark that carries the brand more than the icon. The AP ligature is a Stripe-or-Linear-grade craft detail.
- **CAPITELLO's system discipline** — the 1.2u rule as primitive, the three-weight commitment, the size-responsive optical masters — is in Linear's register. This is the craft Linear specifically is known for.

**Edge: CAPITELLO.** The system-wide rigor and the product-extension (nav active-state as column rule) shows up in the actual interface in a way that software-natives recognize. CIFRA is more *print-era-adapted-to-screen*; CAPITELLO is *screen-native with printed gravitas.*

### 3.4 Apice Test — *Which one* is *Apice?*

This is the defining test. Apice is:

- Italian by name (both directions honor this)
- Premium by price (both can carry $149.90)
- Warm by differentiation (both pair with Warm Cream)
- Disciplined by voice (both are committed systems)
- A **wealth-building practice, not a trading platform** (the Apice Code)

The deciding factor: **Apice is a practice.** The product is not a destination the user arrives at; it is a weekly discipline they inhabit. A seal — a stamp — reads as a *moment*. A column — a monument — reads as a *presence*.

Apice is present in the user's life every week, one DCA contribution at a time. The system should feel like a *presence* more than a *moment*. This pushes toward CAPITELLO.

But — CIFRA's ledger metaphor speaks to *the practice itself*. Every week, a new entry in the ledger. The ledger rule as system primitive is *the product experience made visible.* This pushes toward CIFRA.

The tie-breaker is the CEO's stated vocabulary: **"elite investor."** Elite is a posture of *civic confidence*, not private-banking *discretion*. CAPITELLO's monumentality matches "elite." CIFRA's ledger discretion matches "disciplined" but not quite "elite."

**Edge: CAPITELLO.**

Ship CAPITELLO.
