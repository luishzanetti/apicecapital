# Apice — Tagline Library (by surface)

> **Author:** Stefan Georgi
> **Status:** Production. Use for all 12 surfaces listed.
> **Last updated:** 2026-04-17
> **Rule:** Every tagline below is ≤ 8 words. Every one clears the Voice Audit (`brand-voice-FINAL.md` §7).

## The 12 Product Surfaces

### 1. App Store listing — primary hook (40 chars max)

**Tagline:** *"Build wealth. One week at a time."*
- **Characters:** 34 — fits under the 40-char App Store subtitle limit.
- **Why it works here:** The master tagline earns its place on the most discovered surface. Search-indexed by Apple/Google for "build wealth" and "wealth app" keyword clusters. No wasted word.
- **Paired visually with:** Static lockup (wordmark + emerald dot), warm cream `#F7F3ED` background, zero ornament. The App Store preview video opens on this line before the product footage.

---

### 2. Website homepage hero H1

**Tagline:** *"Wealth, built weekly."*
- **Why it works here:** The website homepage is the one surface where we *compress* the master tagline into a scroll-stopping three-word H1. Longer copy lives in the subhead below it (*"DCA, managed trade, and an Academy that respects your time. From $10 a week."*). The H1 is the pure claim.
- **Paired visually with:** Wordmark + dot motion — the emerald period pulses once at page load, then settles. Behind it: quiet gradient, near-black `#0E0E12` base.

---

### 3. Club tier marketing page — hero

**Tagline:** *"Elite is a habit."*
- **Why it works here:** The Club tier is ideology-first pricing. "Elite is a habit" is the ideology in three words. It removes the transactional frame (what you pay, what you get) and puts the posture first — which is exactly what Club members are buying.
- **Paired visually with:** Full-bleed near-black canvas, wordmark in emerald for this page only (rare inversion), the tagline in display size (~72pt on desktop). Minimal UI below the fold.

---

### 4. Onboarding welcome screen

**Tagline:** *"Welcome. Let's start with $10."*
- **Why it works here:** First-touch onboarding needs warmth and specificity. "Welcome" earns trust; "$10" removes the pricing anxiety before it can form. Six words. No exclamation mark. Pure voice DNA in one line.
- **Paired visually with:** Splash screen — wordmark fades in, dot pulses once, tagline appears below after 300ms. No character, no illustration. Just the line.

---

### 5. First-DCA completion celebration

**Tagline:** *"Week one is the hardest."*
- **Why it works here:** Every other app would say *"You did it!"* — we do not. This line reframes the moment from celebration to *accomplishment of the hardest thing*. It sets up the streak. It also makes the user feel seen — starting *is* the hardest part, and we named it.
- **Paired visually with:** Celebration screen — emerald dot scales up briefly, then returns to position. Streak counter fades in below: *"Week 1 of forever."*

---

### 6. Level-up moment (generic)

**Tagline:** *"You earned this."*
- **Why it works here:** Three words. No "Congratulations!" — that is gaudy. "You earned this" reads like a mentor's nod. It also respects that the user did the work — we did not give them anything.
- **Paired visually with:** Level badge center screen, tagline below. Brief emerald accent on the badge border. Sound: one soft chime, not a fanfare.

---

### 7. Elite Challenge (Challenge 10) completion

**Tagline:** *"You are the Apice."*
- **Why it works here:** This is the signature phrase from the manifesto. It appears *only* on this one surface in the entire product. Because it is rare, it lands with the weight the manifesto earned it. Anywhere else it would be cheapened.
- **Paired visually with:** Framed artifact layout — the user's name, date of completion, and this line. The manifesto text below, printable. The wordmark + dot in emerald as the signature. Designed to be saved as a PDF and printed.

---

### 8. Push notification — weekly streak reached

**Tagline:** *"Week 12. Still building."*
- **Why it works here:** The streak number is the fact. "Still building" is the identity confirmation. No exclamation, no "amazing," no "keep it up." The user is an investor — we talk to them like one.
- **Paired visually with:** Push has no visual — the title is the fact (*"Week 12. Still building."*), the body is the detail (*"+$300 contributed. +$62 unrealized."*). Lock screen legible.

---

### 9. Email — Monday morning motivation

**Tagline:** *"Monday. One week closer."*
- **Why it works here:** DCA fires on Mondays. This is the weekly hero email subject line. Four words. Names the day (so the user knows why it arrived now) and the outcome (closer to wealth). Opens a 200-word email with the week's principle + action.
- **Paired visually with:** Email header — wordmark + dot in emerald, subject line below, one-sentence lede, then content. No hero image. Text-first respect.

---

### 10. Cold ad copy — Meta Reels, first 3 seconds

**Tagline:** *"Stop trading. Start building."*
- **Why it works here:** Cold ads need a *pattern interrupt* — the user is scrolling Reels, we have 3 seconds. "Stop trading. Start building" is the counter-positioning claim in five words. It directly attacks the gambling-crypto frame our real ICP is tired of. Works without prior brand knowledge.
- **Paired visually with:** Reels-native vertical video — first frame is wordmark + dot alone on warm cream. The tagline appears in 700 weight. Cuts to the actual product footage at second 3. No captions over the logo moment.

---

### 11. LinkedIn / professional context

**Tagline:** *"Disciplined wealth, built weekly."*
- **Why it works here:** LinkedIn audience is professional — they recognize "disciplined" as signal, not fluff. This variant trades the warmth of the master tagline for the gravity professional readers expect. Still passes Voice Audit. Still ≤ 8 words.
- **Paired visually with:** LinkedIn company banner — wordmark + dot on near-black, tagline in tracked caps below at small size. Founder's LinkedIn headline uses this line verbatim.

---

### 12. In-app footer / About page

**Tagline:** *"Apice. Wealth, built weekly."*
- **Why it works here:** The about-page / footer surface is the last line before legal text. It repeats the brand name + the three-word compression for final recall. Short enough to live in a single line across device widths.
- **Paired visually with:** Footer — wordmark at small size (left), the tagline as body text (right), ©2026 below. Geist 400, no ornament. The quiet end of every screen.

## Cross-Surface Rules

### Tagline hierarchy (when two might compete)

1. **Master tagline** — *"Build Wealth. One Week at a Time."* — always wins on App Store, email signature, press releases, ad copy over 10 seconds.
2. **Surface variant** (from this library) — wins on surfaces where the master tagline's length would fight the UI (homepage hero, push, level-up moments).
3. **Campaign tagline** (from `brand-voice-FINAL.md` §6.3) — time-boxed, retires after campaign.

### Never do this

- Never stack two taglines on the same surface.
- Never translate taglines without a localization review — *"Wealth, built weekly"* is crisp in EN, dull in ES (*"Riqueza, construida semanalmente"* loses rhythm). Each locale gets its own library.
- Never treat these as interchangeable. Each is hand-fitted to its surface.
