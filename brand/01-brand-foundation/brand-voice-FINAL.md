## 0. What this document is

A private bank built on software does not sound like a crypto app. It sounds like a disciplined operator who refuses to waste your attention.

That voice is defined here. Every writer, designer, PM, and AI copy generator — including the six AI Experts — ships nothing that has not cleared this guide. Voice is not a filter applied at the end. It is the thing you wrote.

The brand name is **Apice**. Not "Apice Capital." Not "Apice." with the period spoken aloud. Product-facing copy says *Apice*. Formal and legal contexts may use *Apice Capital*. The signature period lives inside the wordmark — it is visual, not grammatical.

## 1. Voice DNA — The Four Adjectives

Apice speaks with exactly four traits. Every piece of copy carries all four. Missing one means rewriting.

| Adjective | Means | Does NOT mean |
|---|---|---|
| **Confident** | We know how wealth is built. We state it plainly. | Cocky, macho, "alpha," chest-beating |
| **Warm** | Human to human, never bank to account | Cute, casual, "Hey bestie," emoji-forward |
| **Disciplined** | Every word earns its place. Nothing filler. | Cold, robotic, corporate, legalese |
| **Clear** | One idea per sentence. Numbers say what they mean. | Dumbed-down, oversimplified, patronizing |

**V3.01 register note:** the minimalism of the wordmark extends to the voice. The old logo rounds (v2/v3/v4) invited a slightly louder voice. The final mark is quieter — so the voice is quieter too. *Restraint is the register.*

**Mnemonic:** *Confident · Warm · Disciplined · Clear.* If a line is missing one, rewrite it.

## 2. Tone in 6 Contexts

Voice is the constant. Tone is what we dial up or down by context.

### 2.1 Onboarding — *Inviting*

The reader is deciding whether to trust us. We are gracious, grounded, and make the first step feel small.

- **Dial:** warmth ↑↑ · confidence → · discipline → · clarity ↑
- **Sentence length:** short. Generous whitespace.
- **Example:** *"Welcome. You are about to build something most people never will. Let's start with $10."*

### 2.2 Education — *Illuminating*

Patient teacher, respected student. We explain. We do not lecture.

- **Dial:** confidence ↑ · clarity ↑↑ · warmth → · discipline →
- **Structure:** principle → example → application.
- **Example:** *"Compounding is not magic. It is patience multiplied by time. Here is what $25 a week becomes in ten years."*

### 2.3 Celebration — *Proud, never gaudy*

Level-ups, badges, streaks. We acknowledge like a mentor, not a carnival.

- **Dial:** confidence ↑↑ · warmth ↑ · discipline ↑↑ (restraint matters most here)
- **Forbidden:** emoji stacks, "Woohoo!", multiple exclamation marks, confetti language.
- **Example:** *"Level 4 — Strategist. You have earned this. ALTIS preview unlocks now."*

### 2.4 Caution — *Calm, reassuring*

Market crashes. Low funds. Failed transfers. Most apps panic users here. We do the opposite.

- **Dial:** discipline ↑↑ · warmth ↑↑ · confidence ↑ · clarity ↑↑
- **Rule:** Name what happened. State what to do. Stop.
- **Example:** *"Your DCA fund runs low in 3 weeks. Add $75 before Monday to stay on schedule."*

### 2.5 Upgrade — *Aspirational, never pushy*

Pro and Club tiers are doors, not walls. We describe what is on the other side. No countdowns. No guilt.

- **Dial:** confidence ↑↑ · warmth → · discipline ↑ · clarity ↑
- **Rule:** One benefit per line. End with a quiet invitation, not a scream.
- **Example:** *"Club opens ALTIS, the Elite Mindset track, and quarterly Capital Game seasons. $149.90/month. Cancel whenever you choose."*

### 2.6 Commercial / Marketing — *Confident without hype* ⚡ NEW

Ads, website copy, App Store, cold reels, partnership decks. This is where most fintechs collapse into feature-list noise. We do not.

- **Dial:** confidence ↑↑ · warmth ↑ · discipline ↑↑ · clarity ↑↑
- **Rule:** Lead with the product promise. Never list features in ad copy. The benefit is the headline — the mechanism is the proof below it.
- **Forbidden:** *"Try it now!"*, *"Don't miss out!"*, *"Limited time!"*, *"Join 50,000+ smart investors!"*
- **Permitted:** quiet claims, real numbers, the tagline, the signature phrases.
- **Example (ad headline):** *"Build wealth. One week at a time."* → *"DCA, managed trade, and an Academy that actually respects your time. From $10 a week."*

## 3. The Seven Voice Principles

Every piece of Apice copy honors these. No exceptions.

1. **No gambling-crypto vocabulary.** Forbidden forever: *moon, lambo, ape, degen, YOLO, diamond hands, pump, shill, bag, printing money.* We write like investors, not traders on Telegram.
2. **Numbers are sacred.** Financial figures use tabular alignment and monospaced type. Decimals are consistent (2 for USD, up to 8 for crypto). We never round to look bigger. We never hide what we owe.
3. **One idea per sentence.** If it contains "and," ask whether it should be two sentences. Usually yes.
4. **Name the risk.** When we talk about returns, we talk about drawdowns. Apice does not hide what can go wrong. That is why users trust us.
5. **Address the reader as "you."** "You" is the most powerful word we own. We speak *to* the reader, never *about* them.
6. **Retire the exclamation mark.** Confidence does not yell. One exclamation per screen is the ceiling. Zero is usually better.
7. **Borrow the weight of old money.** Write as if the copy will be printed, framed, and read in ten years. If it looks cheap on paper, it is cheap on screen.

## 5. Signature Phrases — Five Lines That Live Across the Product

These appear recurrently — in onboarding, Academy, emails, App Store, push copy. They *are* the vocabulary of the brand. Every one passes the "framed on paper" test.

1. **"Build wealth. One week at a time."**
   The core promise. Every user sees it the first time they open the app. Also the ad headline, the App Store subtitle, and the email signature.

2. **"Consistency is the compound."**
   The thesis of DCA, Academy, streaks, and the Capital Game — in four words.

3. **"Elite is a habit."**
   The ideology. Used for Club marketing, the Elite Mindset track, and achievement surfaces above Level 4.

4. **"A plan you can keep."**
   The respect clause. Used in onboarding, caution copy, and the risk disclosure. Says: *we design for your real life, not a fantasy.*

5. **"You are the Apice."**
   The initiation. Earned after Elite Mindset track L7. The signature of the house. Never used as a generic CTA — it is a line you unlock.

## 6. Tagline System

### 6.1 Master tagline (locked)

**"Build Wealth. One Week at a Time."**

- **Rationale:** Dropping *Crypto* (from the original "Build Crypto Wealth...") elevates the ceiling from crypto app to wealth platform. Confirms the product roadmap: DCA, AI Trade, Academy, cashback, future equities.
- **Rhythm:** two strong beats, not three. Faster to read. Easier to remember.
- **Pairing:** Always presented with the wordmark + emerald dot. Never alone. Never in all caps.

### 6.2 The five alternatives (held in reserve)

| # | Tagline | When it comes out |
|---|---|---|
| A | **Build Wealth. One Week at a Time.** | Default. Locked. |
| B | **Wealth, Compounded Weekly.** | Editorial / long-form piece headline. |
| C | **The Quiet Way to Build Wealth.** | Campaign counter-positioning against gambling-crypto. |
| D | **Your Weekly Edge.** | B2B partner decks, fintech press. |
| E | **Elite is a Habit.** | Club tier marketing exclusively. |

### 6.3 Campaign variations — approved seasonal / moment-based hooks

These extend the master tagline rhythm. They are not replacements. They appear for specific moments, then retire.

| Moment | Variation | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Club tier unlock** | **"Elite is a Habit. Make it Weekly."** | Club landing page, Club upsell email, upgrade modal. |
| **Elite Mindset Track 6 completion (L7)** | **"You Built This. One Week at a Time."** | Completion certificate, framed artifact, email. |
| **First-DCA celebration** | **"Week one is the hardest."** | In-app celebration screen after first DCA fires. |

These variations respect the master tagline's two-beat rhythm. No campaign line ever exceeds eight words.

## 7. Voice Audit Checklist (12 questions)

Before any copy ships — UI, push, email, ad, App Store, manifesto, partner deck — it clears all twelve.

- [ ] 1. Does it sound **confident** without being cocky?
- [ ] 2. Does it sound **warm** without being cute or casual?
- [ ] 3. Does every sentence earn its place? (**Disciplined** — cut filler.)
- [ ] 4. Is there one idea per sentence? (**Clear**.)
- [ ] 5. Does it address the reader as **you**?
- [ ] 6. Are all numbers **precise and tabular** (no rounding, no dropped decimals)?
- [ ] 7. Is it **free of forbidden lexicon** (see §4)?
- [ ] 8. Would this copy look **credible printed on paper** at a private bank?
- [ ] 9. Does the **exclamation mark count** stay at zero or one?
- [ ] 10. If the copy names a return, does it **also name the risk**?
- [ ] 11. Does it **match the V3.01 register** — Stripe / Linear / Plaid minimalism, not Robinhood exuberance?
- [ ] 12. If read aloud in one breath, does it **hold together** — or does it sound like committee copy?

If any answer is "no" — rewrite.

## 8. Push Notification Voice Guide

Push is the hardest surface — five to twelve words, no preamble, one tap from being closed. Ten examples, final voice:

### 8.1 DCA executed
> **Title:** Your weekly DCA fired.
> **Body:** $25 into BTC at 06:00. Streak: 12 weeks.

### 8.2 Low funds
> **Title:** Your DCA fund runs low.
> **Body:** Add $75 before Monday to stay on schedule.

### 8.3 Market event (sharp drawdown)
> **Title:** The market dropped 6% overnight.
> **Body:** Your plan already accounts for this. Nothing to do.

### 8.4 Achievement unlock
> **Title:** Level 4 — Strategist.
> **Body:** You earned it. ALTIS preview is open.

### 8.5 Weekly summary
> **Title:** Week 18. Still building.
> **Body:** +$125 contributed. +$47 unrealized. One week closer.

### 8.6 Education nudge
> **Title:** Three minutes. One principle.
> **Body:** Why position size outperforms conviction. Track 2, Lesson 4.

### 8.7 Referral reminder
> **Title:** Two friends away from Founding Member.
> **Body:** Share your link. They start at $10. You both keep the discount.

### 8.8 Club upsell
> **Title:** You have the habits. Club has the system.
> **Body:** ALTIS, Elite Mindset, and Capital Game. $149.90/month. Cancel anytime.

### 8.9 Maintenance window
> **Title:** Apice pauses for 20 minutes at 02:00 UTC.
> **Body:** Scheduled maintenance. Your DCA on Monday is not affected.

### 8.10 Security alert
> **Title:** New sign-in from Chrome, São Paulo.
> **Body:** If this was you, nothing to do. If not, tap to lock your account.

**Rules across all ten:**
- Title is the fact. Body is the action or the context.
- No emoji in pushes, ever.
- No exclamation marks.
- Numbers are precise (never "$25-ish" or "about 12 weeks").
- Every push passes the Voice Audit.
