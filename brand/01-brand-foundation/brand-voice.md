## 0. Why this document exists

Apice Capital does not sound like a crypto app. It sounds like a private bank for people who refuse to stay poor.

Every word the product ships — from a push notification to an error state to a Club upsell — should feel like it came from the same confident, disciplined voice. That voice is defined here.

This guide is practical. Writers, designers, PMs, and AI copy generators (including the 6 AI Experts) reference this document before shipping any text.

---

## 1. Voice DNA — The Four Adjectives

Apice speaks with exactly four traits. Every piece of copy must carry all four.

| Adjective | Means | Does NOT mean |
|---|---|---|
| **Confident** | We know how wealth is built. We state it plainly. | Cocky, macho, "alpha," chest-beating |
| **Warm** | We speak human to human, not bank to account | Cute, casual, chummy, "Hey bestie" |
| **Disciplined** | Every sentence earns its place. No filler. | Cold, robotic, corporate, legalese |
| **Clear** | One idea per sentence. Numbers say what they mean. | Dumbed-down, oversimplified, patronizing |

**Mnemonic for writers:** *Confident · Warm · Disciplined · Clear* — if a line is missing any of the four, rewrite it.

## 2. Tone Spectrum — How Voice Shifts by Context

Voice is the constant. Tone is what we dial up or down depending on the moment.

### 2.1 Onboarding — *Inviting*
The reader is deciding whether to trust us. We are gracious, grounded, and make the first step feel small.
- **Dial:** warmth ↑↑, discipline ↓, confidence →
- **Sentence length:** short. Generous whitespace.
- **Example:**
  > "Welcome. You are about to build something most people never will. Let's start with $10."

### 2.2 Education — *Illuminating*
We are a patient teacher who respects the student. We explain, we do not lecture.
- **Dial:** warmth →, discipline →, confidence ↑
- **Structure:** principle → example → application.
- **Example:**
  > "Compounding is not magic. It is patience multiplied by time. Here is what $25 a week becomes in ten years."

### 2.3 Celebration — *Proud, never gaudy*
When a user levels up, earns a badge, or hits a streak milestone — we acknowledge it like a mentor, not a carnival.
- **Dial:** warmth ↑, confidence ↑↑, discipline ↑ (restraint matters)
- **Avoid:** emoji stacks, "Woohoo!", "Amazing job!!"
- **Example:**
  > "Level 4 — Strategist. You have earned this. ALTIS preview unlocks now."

### 2.4 Caution — *Calm, reassuring*
Market crashes. Low funds. Failed transfers. This is where most apps panic users. We do the opposite.
- **Dial:** warmth ↑↑, confidence ↑, discipline ↑↑
- **Rule:** Name what happened. State what to do. Stop.
- **Example:**
  > "Your DCA fund runs low in 3 weeks. Add $75 before Monday to stay on schedule."

### 2.5 Upgrade / Commercial — *Aspirational, never pushy*
The Pro and Club tiers are doors — not walls. We describe what is on the other side. We do not block with countdown timers or guilt.
- **Dial:** confidence ↑↑, warmth →, discipline ↑
- **Rule:** One benefit per line. End with a quiet invitation, not a scream.
- **Example:**
  > "Club opens ALTIS, the Elite Mindset track, and quarterly Capital Game seasons. $149.90/month. Cancel whenever you choose."

## 3. Voice Principles — The Seven Rules

Every piece of Apice copy must honor these. No exceptions.

1. **We never use gambling-crypto language.** Forbidden forever: *moon, lambo, ape, degen, YOLO, diamond hands (as slogan), pump, shill, bag.* We write like investors, not traders on Telegram.
2. **Numbers are sacred.** Financial figures always use tabular alignment and appear in mono-spaced type. Decimals are consistent (2 for USD, up to 8 for crypto). We never round to look bigger.
3. **One idea per sentence.** If a sentence contains "and," ask whether it should be two sentences. Usually yes.
4. **We name the risk.** When we talk about returns, we also talk about drawdowns. Apice does not hide what can go wrong — that is why users trust us.
5. **We use the second person.** "You" is the most powerful word we own. We speak to the reader, never about them.
6. **We retire exclamation marks.** Confidence does not yell. One exclamation per screen is the ceiling; zero is usually better.
7. **We borrow the weight of old money.** Write as if the copy will be printed and framed. If it would look cheap on paper, it is cheap on screen.

## 6. Tagline — Current vs. Proposed

**Current tagline:** *"Build Crypto Wealth. One Week at a Time."*

### Five alternatives for evaluation

| # | Tagline | Posture | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | **Build Wealth. One Week at a Time.** | Broader, more premium | Drops the "crypto" specificity — may reduce ad CTR |
| B | **Wealth, Compounded Weekly.** | Elegant, quieter | Less CTA, more manifesto |
| C | **The Quiet Way to Build Crypto Wealth.** | Counter-positions gambling-crypto directly | Longer, category-dependent |
| D | **Your Weekly Edge.** | Action-oriented, short | May read generic without context |
| E | **Elite Is a Habit.** | Pure brand, ideology-led | Does not say what the product does |

### Recommendation: **Evolve to Option A — "Build Wealth. One Week at a Time."**

**Reasoning:**
- The word *crypto* in the tagline caps the brand's ceiling at "crypto app." Apice is positioning to be a wealth-building platform that happens to use crypto rails — not a crypto novelty.
- Dropping "Crypto" signals confidence (we do not need to justify our asset class) and future-proofs the brand (stablecoins, tokenized assets, potentially equities later).
- The rhythm is tighter: two strong beats instead of three.
- Users still find us under "crypto investing" keywords via ASO and ad copy — the tagline does not need to carry that job.
- Option E ("Elite Is a Habit") is held in reserve as a **campaign tagline** for the Club tier, not the master brand line.

**Keep the current tagline only if** the CEO's research shows that "crypto" is still a primary trust-anchor keyword for the EN-first ICP. This is a judgment call for Morgan (@pm) and Atlas (@analyst) to weigh in on.

## 7. Voice Audit Checklist

Before any copy ships — UI, push, email, ad, App Store — it must pass all eight.

- [ ] 1. Does it sound **confident** without being cocky?
- [ ] 2. Does it sound **warm** without being cute or casual?
- [ ] 3. Does every sentence earn its place? (**Disciplined** — cut filler.)
- [ ] 4. Is there one idea per sentence? (**Clear**.)
- [ ] 5. Does it address the reader as **you**?
- [ ] 6. Are all numbers **precise and tabular** (no rounding, no dropped decimals)?
- [ ] 7. Is it **free of forbidden lexicon** (moon, lambo, ape, hack, guaranteed, don't miss out)?
- [ ] 8. Would this copy look **credible printed on paper** at a private bank?

If the answer to any of these is "no" — rewrite. Voice is not a filter you apply at the end. It is the thing you wrote.
