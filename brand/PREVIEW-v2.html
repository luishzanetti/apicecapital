Direction 1

Ascent

Benchmark: Nike

"The gesture of going up, in a single unbroken stroke."

A variable-weight stroke rising from lower-left to upper-right. Heavy at the start, thins as it climbs. The mark doesn't point at a summit — it is the climb. One bezier, no metaphor, no letter.

Pros

  • Purest "one gesture"
  • Journey-line thesis built-in
  • Rarest in category
  • Animates in 900ms

Cons

  • Needs uniform-weight variant at 16px
  • May read as graphic before brand (Nike decade-one)
Risk: LOWProd: 5–7 daysFavicon: good (variant)
Direction 2

Summit

Benchmark: Apple

"One filled shape. A peak, asymmetric by one degree."

A convex rounded triangle with peak offset right (+2u) and baseline tilt (lower-right 1u above lower-left). One filled form. No stroke. Survives embroidery, engraving, newspaper ink.

Pros

  • Best favicon of the 3 (no variant)
  • Lowest cognitive cost
  • Physical-medium ready
  • Most "wealth-coded"

Cons

  • Peak-as-logo is crowded — execution has to be flawless
  • Implies destination, not motion
Risk: MEDIUMProd: 4–6 daysFavicon: excellent
Direction 3

Compass

Benchmark: Claude

"A 4-point star, hand-drawn rather than compass-drawn."

Four arms with mixed terminals (N+E sharp, S+W rounded). Vertical pair heavier than horizontal. The asymmetry IS the warmth signal. Extends natively to the 6 AI Experts system.

Pros

  • Richest semantic surface
  • Native AI Experts extensibility
  • Hand-drawn warmth

Cons

  • Worst favicon (needs separate variant)
  • Risk of "AI sparkle" misread
  • Tightest execution tolerance
Risk: HIGHProd: 10–14 daysFavicon: poor
