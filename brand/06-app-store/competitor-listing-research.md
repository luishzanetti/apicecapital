## 0. Methodology & calibration

We audited 10 competitors selected on three axes:

- **Investing incumbents** (Robinhood, Public.com, Wealthfront, Betterment, Acorns)
- **Crypto-native platforms** (Coinbase, Nexo, Swan Bitcoin)
- **Neobanks that bolt investing on** (Cash App / Block, Revolut)

For each, we recorded: App name (≤30), Subtitle (≤30), Description opening hook (first ~120 chars), Feature structure, Social proof cues, Category ranking, and inferred keyword strategy. We then synthesized five strategic insights relevant to Apice's EN-first positioning.

**Two critical platform facts (2026) shaping all research:**

1. **Apple screenshot caption indexing (June 2025 shift):** Apple now extracts text from screenshot captions and treats it as indexed keyword metadata. Screenshots are no longer pure conversion — they now drive discovery. This triples the ASO surface area from 160 characters (Name + Subtitle + Keyword field) to include caption copy across 7–10 screenshots.
2. **First three screenshots drive 90% of install decisions** and appear directly in search results. Caption-level keyword density in those three frames is now a top-three ASO lever.

Source: Appfigures ASO research (2025–2026), Growth by Kev ASO Best Practices 2026.

## 1. Robinhood — "Investing for All"

**App name:** *Robinhood: Trading & Investing* (29 chars — uses full budget)
**Subtitle:** *BTC, Crypto, Stocks & Options.* (30 chars — keyword-stuffed)
**Category:** Finance — historically **#1 in Finance**, consistently top-5 in 2025–26
**Ratings:** 4.3 stars | 4.7M ratings (US)
**Opening hook (first ~120 chars):**
> "Robinhood helps you run your money your way. Identify trends for your investing strategies with technical indicators like moving average (MA), relative strength index (RSI), and more."

**Description structure:**
- TRADING (commission-free stocks, ETFs, options)
- ROBINHOOD GOLD ($5/month — APY 3.35%, instant deposits, margin)
- PREDICTION MARKETS (new product line)
- Regulatory disclosures at bottom

**Feature callouts:** Custom price alerts, advanced charts, 24/5 stock trading, Apple Watch support, Gold subscription, prediction markets.

**Screenshot sequence (inferred from Appfigures teardown + current listing):**
1. Hero — portfolio gains + "Investing for everyone" line
2. Crypto buy flow
3. Gold APY hero
4. Options chain
5. Charts/indicators
6. News + research feed

**Social proof in listing:** 4.7M ratings is the proof. No "As seen in" blocks. Letting scale speak.

**Inferred keyword list** (from Appfigures teardown, extrapolated):
`trade,trading,trader,stock,buy,bitcoin,crypto,market,tracker,ethereum,wallet,brokerage,invest,options,prediction`

**What they do well:**
- Subtitle uses every comma-separated keyword ("BTC, Crypto, Stocks & Options")
- Keyword list and subtitle overlap is minimal (good ASO hygiene)
- $5/month Gold price embedded in description — low mental cost, discoverable via search

**What Apice can learn / avoid:**
- Robinhood is the incumbent; head-on keyword war is unwinnable (they own "investing"). Apice must flank on **DCA, automation, education** where Robinhood is weak.
- Subtitle is loud but generic — Apice can win on specificity ("Build wealth. Every week.")
- Robinhood's voice is flat-transactional. Premium/warm voice is a wide-open lane.

## 12. Five strategic insights for Apice's App Store presence

### Insight 1 — The "Wealth" keyword is contested. "Wealth weekly" is open.

Wealthfront + Nexo ("Crypto Wealth Platform") + Swan ("generational Bitcoin wealth") all fight for the word **wealth**. None of them own the phrase **wealth weekly** or **weekly wealth** or **one week at a time**.

Apice's tagline (*"Build wealth. One week at a time."*) is not just a brand line — it is an **unclaimed keyword cluster**. We should engineer the subtitle + screenshot captions + keyword field to reinforce this cluster so we rank for "weekly investing," "weekly DCA," "build wealth weekly," and long-tail "invest $25 weekly."

This is the ASO white space Apice can actually win.

### Insight 2 — The first 3 screenshot captions are now indexed keyword real estate.

The June 2025 Apple algorithm shift means screenshot caption text is indexed. Nine of ten competitors in this study have NOT updated their screenshots for this shift (most still use passive captions like "Easy to Use" or pure product imagery). Apice ships brand-new, so every caption can be ASO-engineered from day one.

**Recommendation:** Every caption must contain exactly one high-intent keyword phrase (e.g., "Automated weekly DCA," "Learn as you earn," "6 AI experts, one decision"). This is covered in detail in `screenshot-brief.md` and `aso-strategy.md`.

### Insight 3 — "Wealthfront" collision on "build wealth."

Wealthfront's taglines ("Build wealth better", "built to help you build wealth") and our "Build wealth. One week at a time." overlap on the verb+noun core. This is not fatal — Apple does not penalize phrase overlap between independent apps — but it does mean **Apice must win the distinction in the modifier** (the "one week at a time" frequency phrase).

**Practical implication:** Subtitle should contain the frequency marker ("Every week," "Weekly," or "One week at a time"). Keyword field should include `weekly,dca,recurring,compound,habit` to separate us from Wealthfront's robo/ETF cluster.

### Insight 4 — "Education + DCA + AI Experts" is a novel triple Apice owns.

Only Public.com approaches this combination (Alpha AI + Investment Plans + Town Halls), and Public underweights each. No competitor in the set has:
- Named, personality-distinct AI experts (6)
- Progressive gamified education (7 tracks, 52 lessons, 6 levels) tied to product activation
- DCA automation that drives both onboarding AND retention via streaks

**Recommendation:** The App Store screenshots and description should lead with this triple, not with generic crypto/investing claims. This is the single most defensible positioning Apice has.

### Insight 5 — Pricing is the unresolved tension.

Apice's current product pricing ($49.90 / $149.90) is 5–10× Acorns ($3–$9), ~10× Robinhood Gold ($5), ~6× Wealthfront (no subscription; fees on AUM), and in the range of Masterclass ($15/mo) and Bloomberg ($35/mo). At $49.90/mo the listing must communicate "this is premium education + AI + automation, not just DCA" — otherwise users will bounce to the cheaper competitors.

Three options to resolve (detail in `aso-strategy.md §5` and flagged to Jarvis):

1. **Ship listing at full product pricing** ($49.90 / $149.90) and bet on premium positioning carrying the conversion.
2. **Introduce App Store-specific tiers** at $4.99/$14.99 (closer to Acorns/Robinhood Gold) with content/feature overlap but web-gated depth.
3. **Obfuscate pricing in the listing** — don't mention price in description, defer to paywall after onboarding (riskier; reduces informed-conversion).

Recommendation pending CEO decision. This is the single biggest unresolved listing question.
