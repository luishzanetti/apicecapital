## 0. North Star

Apice is not selling crypto exposure. Apice is selling **a weekly ritual of becoming a better investor**. Every campaign must reinforce three things:

1. **Discipline compounds** — the product is the habit, the habit is the product.
2. **Respect for the user's time** — no noise, no dopamine drip, no laser eyes.
3. **Premium-accessible** — opens at free DCA, scales to Club at $149.90. The premium ceiling is aspirational, not gatekept.

**The category sells the rush. Apice sells the rhythm.**

## 1. Three primary campaign archetypes

### Archetype A — "The Weekly Habit"
**Value prop:** *Consistency beats brilliance. Weekly DCA is the most under-rated wealth-building tool of the decade.*

- **Target:** DCA-curious retail (25–45). Has savings, wants exposure, afraid of timing the market. Likely listens to finance podcasts but never pulled the trigger.
- **Core insight:** They don't fail because they don't know. They fail because they don't *show up*. Apice solves showing-up.
- **Creative angle:** Calm, editorial, stat-driven. Show the *boring* truth: $50/week for 10 years at 8% = $41k. No charts screaming. No hype.
- **Proof engine:** Compounding calculator, streak leaderboard, lesson completion data.
- **Voice:** Matter-of-fact. "This is how it works. Start Monday."
- **Primary channels:** Meta (feed + story), YouTube pre-roll, Organic IG/LinkedIn, Email.
- **Visual mood:** Cream base, dark text, single emerald accent. Never motion-heavy.
- **KPIs:** CPC < $1.20 · CVR (signup) > 12% · D7 retention > 35% · First-DCA rate (activation) > 18%.

### Archetype B — "The Elite Investor"
**Value prop:** *Club is where the long game is played. Apice Code ideology, private notes, ALTIS, Capital Game.*

- **Target:** Already-investor with $25k+ in deployable capital. Pro/Club prospect. Reads Morgan Housel, owns index funds, wants more structure without more noise.
- **Core insight:** Premium tools exist (Bloomberg, TradingView pro). Premium *ideology* does not. Apice Code owns that.
- **Creative angle:** Manifesto-grade. Black on cream. Typographic. The product as philosophy.
- **Proof engine:** Apice Code quotes, Elite Mindset track, Challenge 10 (Elite), monthly CEO note.
- **Voice:** Declarative. No CTAs that beg. Invitation over persuasion.
- **Primary channels:** LinkedIn (sponsored + organic), YouTube long-form, Email drip to warm list, Organic X/Twitter threads.
- **Visual mood:** Dark background, restrained type, emerald as signature only. Nothing shiny.
- **KPIs:** Club conversion from Pro (90d) > 10% · LinkedIn CPC < $3.50 · Manifesto share rate > 4% · Email click > 7%.

### Archetype C — "The Capital Game"
**Value prop:** *Wealth-building, gamified without gimmicks. Streaks, XP, levels, Capital Game quarterly leaderboard.*

- **Target:** Gen Z / Millennial retail (20–32). Grew up on Duolingo, Robinhood, Strava. Wants to *feel* progress, not just see numbers.
- **Core insight:** They want gamification, not kitsch. Apice gamifies discipline (Strava for wealth), not degen behavior (Robinhood confetti for options).
- **Creative angle:** Streak celebrations, level-ups, XP milestones. But held at editorial temperature. Progress dots. Clean numbers. Never confetti-explosion.
- **Proof engine:** Streak flame tiers (orange → blue → purple → rainbow at 365), Level 6 "Elite" reveal, Capital Game leaderboard.
- **Voice:** Confident-casual. "You kept the week. That's the whole thing."
- **Primary channels:** TikTok, Instagram Reels/Stories, YouTube Shorts, Push notifications, Referrals.
- **Visual mood:** High contrast. Dark + emerald dominant. Bold typographic numbers. Fast cuts allowed here (it's the one place).
- **KPIs:** TikTok CPM < $4 · 7-day streak rate > 22% · Referral K-factor > 0.15 · TikTok → signup CVR > 6%.

## 3. Creative guardrails

### What we ALWAYS do
1. **Restraint over spectacle** — One idea per frame. One color accent. One CTA.
2. **Craft on inspection** — Kerning tight (-30 tracking on headlines). Baselines aligned. Thin rules at 0.10–0.14 opacity for structure.
3. **Data over adjectives** — "$50/week, 10yr, 8% = $41k" beats "life-changing returns."
4. **Editorial typography** — Geist Sans primary. No novelty fonts. Weight as hierarchy, not color.
5. **Cream + dark, emerald as signature** — The palette is identity. Never inverted for "fun."
6. **Speak to the user's intelligence** — Never explain why DCA works. Assume they've read the research. Show the habit instead.
7. **Product as proof** — Screens, numbers, streaks. Not stock photography.

### What we NEVER do
1. **No laser eyes, no diamond hands, no Lambo, no moon** — we are the adult in the room.
2. **No fake urgency** — "24 hours left" / "price going up Monday" is banned. We sell a 10-year habit; countdown timers contradict the thesis.
3. **No "get rich" language** — "wealth," "compounding," "discipline" are our nouns.
4. **No neon gradients, no glow, no 3D logos** — category-rejection list per `visual-research-category-DNA.md` §3.5.
5. **No fear-based creatives** — "Don't miss out" / "everyone's buying" is crypto-bro playbook. Apice sells to those who ignored the 2021 crash AND the 2024 rally.
6. **No influencer-selfie hero shots** — creator content allowed (TikTok) but never as hero on paid feed.
7. **No gambling metaphors** — we are not a casino wrapped in a UI.
8. **No stock photos of suit-men-shaking-hands** — no "diverse group laughing at laptop" stock — category cliché.

### Visual DO — 5 examples
1. **DO:** Big number hero (`$50` / `$41k`) in Geist 700, single emerald accent, cream background. (`instagram-post-2.svg`)
2. **DO:** Manifesto quote in 88px, opening quote mark at 20% emerald opacity as texture. (`instagram-post-3.svg`)
3. **DO:** Streak number at 420px dominating the frame, emerald fill, dark background. (`instagram-story.svg`)
4. **DO:** Thin rule (0.12 opacity) to separate header from body — never boxes, never shadows.
5. **DO:** Bottom-right wordmark stamp on web heroes — signature presence without crowding.

### Visual DON'T — 5 examples
1. **DON'T:** Gradient backgrounds (especially emerald-to-dark fade) — reads Web3.
2. **DON'T:** Crypto coin imagery (BTC icon, ETH icon) on hero placements — shrinks the ceiling.
3. **DON'T:** Photographed smiling faces on paid feed heroes — we compete with Robinhood by looking *less* like Robinhood.
4. **DON'T:** Multi-accent color schemes (emerald + amber + violet) — the system is ONE signature.
5. **DON'T:** Decorative icons (rockets, flames, arrows shooting up) — the streak flame is a product feature, not a marketing garnish.

## 4. Launch sequence — First 90 days

### Pre-launch (Week -4 → Week 0)
**Objective:** Build a waitlist of 5,000 qualified leads. Prime the brand for recognition.

- **Week -4:** Lock brand system + ship 4 OG variants. Email capture page live (`apice.capital/early`). Warm list from CEO's personal network (≤ 300).
- **Week -3:** Launch Instagram + LinkedIn with 6 organic posts (3 manifesto quotes from Archetype B, 2 stat posts from Archetype A, 1 streak preview from C).
- **Week -2:** Soft ad spend — $3K test on Meta + TikTok to calibrate CPL. No direct product pitch yet; sell the waitlist + "Founding Member" future status.
- **Week -1:** Seed 15 creators (finance + discipline tags) with early access in exchange for 1 TikTok + 1 Reel. Launch the Apice Weekly newsletter (see § weekly-content-system) to the waitlist.
- **Week 0:** Public launch eve. Final email to waitlist: "You're in. Code lands 8am." CEO organic thread on X/LinkedIn.

### Launch week (Week 1)
**Objective:** Convert waitlist + capture the news cycle. Target 10k signups.

- **D1:** Open signup. Meta + TikTok + Google Search live at $15K/week combined.
- **D2–D3:** Creator content wave (15 partners hit their feeds). Press embargo lifts on 2 pre-briefed outlets.
- **D4:** Archetype A dominates Meta. First email from Founders: "Here's your first DCA. 90 seconds."
- **D5:** First activation stats shared organically ("X people started. Median first plan = $50/week").
- **D6–D7:** Retargeting kicks in. Non-converters from D1–D5 see Archetype B on LinkedIn.

### Month 1 — DCA Track promotion
**Objective:** Drive first DCA execution (activation) to > 18%. Nail the habit loop.

- Primary creatives: Archetype A feed + square + story.
- Email: 7-day onboarding drip ending on "Complete your first DCA" trigger.
- Organic: push the streak system. Every Monday, resurface a member's 7-day streak as proof.
- Test: A/B between "$10 minimum" and "Start with a week of coffee" hooks.

### Month 2 — Academy + Club tier reveal
**Objective:** Convert free → Pro (target 1.8%), seed Club pipeline.

- Launch Archetype B on LinkedIn. First paid promotion of the Academy track (OG: `og-image-academy.svg`).
- Open Club tier to first 500 Pro users by invite. Leverage scarcity *truthfully* ("500 founding Club members" — a real cap, not a fake urgency device).
- Email: "Your Pro trial is open. Here's why the 7-track Academy exists."
- Organic: manifesto posts hit heaviest in month 2 (once audience knows the brand, they crave the worldview).

### Month 3 — Elite Challenge + Referrals
**Objective:** Activate Challenge 10 narrative. Referral K-factor > 0.15.

- Launch Capital Game quarterly for the first time. OG: branded leaderboard graphic.
- Push Challenge 9 (3 referrals = 1mo Pro free) as hero in-app and in email.
- TikTok Archetype C — level-up celebration videos from real users.
- Month-3 review: CAC stabilization, LTV:CAC > 3:1 is the gate for scale in Month 4+.

## 6. Measurement & attribution

| Metric | Source | Review cadence |
|---|---|---|
| CAC blended | Meta + TikTok + Google Ads API → PostHog | Weekly |
| Signup CVR | PostHog `signup` event | Daily during launch, weekly after |
| First-DCA rate (activation) | PostHog `dca.plan_created` event | Weekly |
| D7 / D30 retention | PostHog cohort | Weekly |
| Free → Pro CVR | Supabase `subscriptions` table | Weekly |
| Pro → Club CVR (90d) | Supabase | Monthly |
| Streak rate (7d / 30d) | `user_xp.streak` column | Weekly |
| Channel ROAS | UTMIFY MCP | Weekly |
| LTV:CAC | Derived — 6wk rolling | Monthly |

**Gate to scale spend:** LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1 on 6-week rolling. Under that, we pause scaling and fix funnel leak (most likely activation, second-most likely D7 retention).
